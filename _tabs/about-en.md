# About

Hello, I’m **BBACK**.

This blog is more than just a place to collect posts. It is an archive of what I’ve actually learned, built, improved, and reflected on over time.

The main topics I cover here include:

- **Resume / Career**: how I have worked, what kinds of problems I have solved, and how my career has evolved
- **Engineering**: backend development, system design, microservices, performance tuning, and operations
- **Study Notes**: concepts, lessons learned, and technical notes worth revisiting later
- **Insights**: practical thoughts and observations from real work and everyday problem-solving
- **Review / Recommendation**: tools, services, gear, and products I have actually used or compared

## Why this blog exists

Memory fades, but written records stay.

That is why I run this blog for a few clear reasons:

1. To organize what I learn so I can revisit it quickly
2. To help others solve similar problems faster
3. To accumulate my own standards and thinking in a transparent way
4. To share useful tools and products in a way that feels natural, not forced

## Writing principles

The writing on this blog follows a few simple principles.

- Write so that readers can **get the answer quickly**
- Include not only strengths, but also **limitations and trade-offs**
- Prioritize **practical information and real usage context** over marketing-style writing
- If affiliate links are included, **disclose them clearly**
