This blog started as a place to collect more than just posts. I wanted it to become an archive of things I have actually learned, built, improved, and found worth sharing over time.

There is no shortage of information on the internet. What is often missing, however, is context: why something matters, who it helps, where it works well, and where it does not. I wanted to keep writing pieces that are useful both to others and to my future self.

That is the direction of this blog.

## What this blog is about

The main topics I plan to cover include:

- backend engineering
- system design
- performance tuning
- study notes and technical learning logs
- resume / career-related writing
- reviews of tools, services, and gear
- recommendation posts based on real usage context

## Why I am writing

I want this blog to serve a few purposes at the same time:

1. make what I learn easier to revisit later
2. help others solve similar problems faster
3. accumulate my own standards and thinking in a transparent way
4. share useful tools and products without turning posts into obvious ads

## Writing principles

This blog tries to stay practical.

- write around real search intent
- answer the question early
- include not only strengths, but also limitations and trade-offs
- prefer useful context over exaggerated claims
- disclose affiliate links clearly when they are included

## What I hope this becomes

Ideally, this blog becomes three things at once:

- a fast answer for someone who searched for a specific problem
- a useful reference point for someone making a decision
- a long-term archive of my own learning and work

I am planning to build it slowly, but consistently.
